Find The Way

Each and every one of us has a unique story to share. Not all of us possess a degree in computer science. However, I have discovered a way to leverage my background in designing, land surveying, geospatial analysis, and web mapping to become a frontend developer.

Crafting Stories with Maps 🎨

You would be so surprised when I tell you that you have to be an artist to work with maps. This is true because you need to tell your story accurately in order to make the right decision.


Cartography taught me the art of selecting typography, colors, and symbols to convey a compelling story within limited space on a map; a single line can make all the difference in telling a vast narrative.

Learning SQL

I learned SQL without realizing that I'm actually using SQL. All GIS software utilizes SQL to execute their special queries, such as finding all customers within 5 km of a specific store or identifying roads that intersect with a river. These queries can all be answered using SQL.


And I learned about (geo)databases within GIS because, just like everything else in GIS, data needs a place to store itself. This place, as you rightly guessed, is a database. But not just any kind of database; you're likely to use a geospatial database. These spatial types of databases are designed to store geospatial data, such as the coordinates of a house, the four bounds, or the starting and ending points of a street (vector line). Understanding how to structure, connect, update, and share these databases instills in me a feeling and passion for databases.

Precision and Communication

In my role at an aerial survey company, I've come to appreciate the art of precision and the importance of meticulous attention to detail. Yet, it's the dynamic interaction with individuals and the art of truly listening that has shaped my journey. From engaging with workers and operators to collaborating closely with managers, I've been immersed in the intricate decision-making processes that drive significant projects forward.


In this realm, where every detail counts, the stakes are high, and a single misstep could unravel a cascade of challenges. Thus, maintaining unwavering focus is not just a requirement but a thrilling challenge in itself.

(ITI) Information Technology Institute

After that, I decided to take a scholarship step in my journey. I prepared myself and applied to the ITI Information Technology Institute, provided by the Ministry of Communication and Information Technology (MCIT). There, I studied web development and GIS. During this scholarship, I found myself incredibly excited about the idea of frontend development.


It was my first exposure to understanding what exactly frontend entails. We created numerous applications and delved into understanding the intricacies of the web and how users interact with it. We learned how to create our own websites and manipulate HTML pages to craft visually appealing content. However, the curriculum didn't delve deep enough, leaving me eager to learn more about this fascinating career.

There is more to Learn

After graduation, I set myself a frontend roadmap. I started with a series of courses, covering core concepts and advancing into new technologies. Each day, my passion for frontend development grew stronger. Believing in learning by doing, I embarked on creating numerous projects. Along the way, I successfully identified my weaknesses and strengths, continuously developing new skills that expanded daily.


Now, I can confidently assert that I am prepared to work in a software development environment, whether as part of a team or as an individual contributor. I have connected the dots and am ready to help create your dream web experience, ranging from simple forms to complex applications and websites, utilizing both simple and advanced modern technologies in 2D or 3D.
